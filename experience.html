This is where employee experience comes into play. Companies nowadays claim to be doing a great job at employee experience. They provide cakes on birthdays or put up quirky banners with fun words. And that's pretty much it. Jacob Morgan points out a fundamental flaw in this perspective. Businesses have assumed that people need to work with them to pay bills. But the world is changing fast. With technology, options like freelancing and content creation are becoming popular. Some people are becoming Uber drivers, while others are making food blogs. On top of it, companies are continuously competing for skilled employees.
